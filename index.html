General Security Incident Steps

  • Document the incident with screenshots
  • Disconnect affected devices from internet
  • Change passwords on unaffected devices
  • Contact your bank for financial fraud
  • Report to local authorities

Digital Security Glossary

Simple explanations of technical terms used in this platform:

A-E

Assessment: A set of questions to help understand your preferences and habits

Authentication: Proving you are who you say you are when logging in

Backup: Making copies of your important files so you don't lose them

Browser: The app you use to visit websites (like Chrome, Safari, or Firefox)

Cloud Storage: Saving your files on the internet instead of just on your device

Encryption: Scrambling your data so only authorised people can read it

F-P

Firewall: Software that blocks unwanted internet traffic from reaching your device

Malware: Harmful software designed to damage or steal from your computer

Multi-factor Authentication (MFA): Using two steps to log in (like password plus text code)

Password Manager: An app that creates and remembers strong passwords for you

Phishing: Fake messages or websites trying to steal your personal information

Privacy Settings: Controls that let you choose who can see your information online

Q-Z

QR Code: Square barcode you scan with your phone camera to open links

Router: The device that brings internet into your home and creates Wi-Fi

Scam: Someone trying to trick you into giving them money or information

Software Update: New version of an app or program that fixes problems and adds security

Two-Factor Authentication: Same as multi-factor - using two ways to prove it's really you

VPN: Virtual Private Network - software that hides your internet activity from others

Wi-Fi: Wireless internet connection in your home or public places
